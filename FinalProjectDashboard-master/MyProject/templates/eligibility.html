Factors Affecting Personal Loan Eligibility


Many factors might affect the personal loan eligibility criteria of borrowers. These factors are as follows:

  • Age: Your age determines the amount of loan that you can avail of. If you are younger, you have a longer working life ahead of you. This factor makes you eligible for a higher amount of personal loan
  • Income: Your monthly net income is directly proportional to your eligibility. The higher the income, the higher would be the loan amount that you can receive.
  • Location: People living in metropolitan cities can get a higher loan amount because of their increased expenses.
  • Type of occupation: Salaried individuals make better borrowers as they have a regular source of income. In the case of self-employed borrowers, the income depends on the business performance and varies.
    As such, salaried employees have easier eligibility requirements than self-employed borrowers. Moreover, the documents required for personal loans are also less for salaried employees.
  • The reputation of the employer: For salaried borrowers, the reputation of their employer also impacts their personal loan eligibility . If you are working with a reputed company, your job is perceived to be stable, making you eligible for a higher loan amount.
  • Existing debts: Existing loans reduce your repayment capacity. As such, they reduce the loan amount that you can avail.
  • Credit score: Lastly, a good credit score, i.e., 700 and above, is needed to avail a personal loan. The credit score shows your creditworthiness. The higher the score, the more eligible you become to avail the loan.
